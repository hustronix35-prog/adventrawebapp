A minimalist trip to the Valley of Wonders!

Sep 10 • Swapnesh Banerjee, All-Weather Runner, Backpacker, Adventurer and Explorer

August 2017. I am sitting in front of a cave, with one of the loveliest places on the planet before me - the Valley of Flowers in Uttarakhand. As I sat there admiring the wonderful view of a valley bedecked with flowers and flanked by majestic mountains, I reflected on the journey that brought me here, 2500km from Bangalore, where I live. For for this particular trip, it was not only the destination that was memorable but the journey itself.

A random remark on a trip with my wife - 'It is possible to travel in India for 500/- a day' had resulted in me accepting the challenge to prove it by taking a trip myself on that budget! I love trekking, so I settled on the Valley of Flowers and Hemkund as a destination - so here is a short account of the experience.

So 5 days back I had taken a train from Bangalore to Delhi, in a sleeper class carriage - I think the ticket costed around Rs 850/-. Now the trip itself took almost two days (44 hours) - and I had negotiated with my wife to allow me to carry food from home for the train journey as well as some chiki :). A word about the train journey - I will still say that if someone has time, a long train ride in India with a book is one of the best ways to spend a day or two. The route from Bangalore to Delhi meanders through the heart of the country and it is always fascinating to see the surroundings and landscapes change as we transition from south to north India.

After 44 hours I reached Dehli. A quick bite of roadside chhole batura satisfied my hunger and then I was off to ISBT Kashmere Gate via metro to catch my bus to Haridwar. Long story short, I reached Haridwar in the evening and then bought a ticket for a bus to Joshmath for next morning. Of course, the main problem to solve was accomodation for the night on my extremely limited budget. I tried at a couple of places but was told that even dharamshalas would charge 400/-. However just as I was thinking maybe my best bet would be the station platform, I spotted a hotel which advertised rooms for 300/-. I waltzed in and started negotiating with the statement that I'll stay in the room only from 10:0pm to 4:00 am and my persistence paid off with me getting a room for Rs 250/-. And ... it was a proper room with a TV and a tiled bathroom to boot.

Anyway, I was off to Joshmath in a local bus early next morning. The road through Joshimath goes through five river confluences and thus has some wonderful spots - especially Rudraprayag where the muddy rust coloured Mandakini cascades down from Kedarnath to meet the pristine green waters of the Alankanda playfully flowing down from Badrinath. I reached Joshimath at around 5pm, just in time to catch the last shuttle jeep to Govindghat, which is the starting point of the trek where I spent the night in a rather 'basic' room - let's leave it at that!

So far I had spent around 1400/- on transportation, 250 on food and 550 on accomodation - so around 2200/ for 4 days, a tad over budget, but I knew that I would make it up in the trek days.

A note regarding the cost of food - I had minimized the breakfast cost by buying a large loaf of bread and some jam for 50/- which sufficed for 4 days - - starting in Haridwar. Even basic dhabas however was not as cheap as I thought because a meal would cost around 80-100. So I ended up eating at shacks where a couple of alu parathas or rajma-chawal was 50/- and hence my daily meals would cost around 100/-. And yes - no tea or coffee!
